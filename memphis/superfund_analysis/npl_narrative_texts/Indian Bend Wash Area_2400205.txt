NPL Site Narrative for Indian Bend Wash
Area
 
INDIAN BEND WASH AREA
Scottsdale, Tempe, Phoenix, Arizona
 
 
Conditions at listing (December 1982) : The Indian Bend Wash Area encompasses about 12 square
miles in parts of Scottsdale, Tempe, and Phoenix, Arizona. Ground water, which supplies more than
350,000 people in the area, is contaminated with trichloroethylene (TCE) and other chlorinated solvents.
Six municipal wells have been shut down as a result of the contamination.
 
Status (July 1983) : An Indian Bend Wash Task Force comprised of EPA, State, county, and municipal
representatives is seeking to identify the extent, magnitude, and sources of the contamination. EPA is
developing a Remedial Action Master Plan outlining the investigations needed to determine the full extent
of cleanup required at the site.
