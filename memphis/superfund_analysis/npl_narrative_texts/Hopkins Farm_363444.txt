NPL Site Narrative for Hopkins Farm
 
HOPKINS FARM
Plumstead Township, New Jersey
 
 
Conditions at listing (September 1983) : The Hopkins Site covers 57 acres in Plumstead Township, New
Jersey. It is one of seven "Plumstead" sites in Ocean and Monmouth Counties, of which four were placed
on the National Priorities List in September 1983. All sites are within about a 20-square-mile rural farming
area. The site was used for disposal of an unknown number of drums, and there is evidence that bulk
liquids were dumped. The State detected heavy metals and other hazardous chemicals in concentrations
above drinking water standards in two of the six monitoring wells on-site. Also, State inspectors noted
chemical odors on-site.
 
Status (June 1984) : EPA is considering various alternatives for the site.
