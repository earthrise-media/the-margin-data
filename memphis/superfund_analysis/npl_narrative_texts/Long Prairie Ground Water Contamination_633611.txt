NPL Site Narrative for Long Prairie
Ground Water Contamination
 
LONG PRAIRIE GROUND WATER CONTAMINATION
Long Prairie, Minnesota
 
 
Conditions at proposal (October 15, 1984) : Various private and municipal wells in Long Prairie
(population 2,900) in Todd County, Minnesota, are contaminated with solvents (dichloroethylene, 1,1,2-
trichloroethane, trichloroethylene, and tetrachloroethylene), according to tests conducted by the State in
late 1983 during routine testing of municipal wells. The State has sampled the wells since then. Ground
water contamination is limited to an area of about 40 acres downgradient from the central downtown area.
The State is conducting a hydrogeologic study to help pinpoint the source of contamination.
 
Status (June 10, 1986) : Under a cooperative agreement with EPA, the State is planning a remedial
investigation/feasibility study to determine the type and extent of contamination at the site and identify
alternatives for remedial action.
