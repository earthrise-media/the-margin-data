NPL Site Narrative for Powell Road
Landfill
 
POWELL ROAD LANDFILL
Dayton, Ohio
 
 
Conditions at listing (September 1983) : Powell Road Landfill covers 67 acres in Dayton, Ohio. The
privately owned operation began in 1959 and is still active. Records indicate that for approximately 2 years
at least 250 drums of wastes, including strontium chromate and benzidine, were delivered to the site every
month. The wastes, in solid, sludge, and liquid form, are toxic, persistent, flammable, and highly volatile.
There is no evidence of the landfill being lined, and some containers are leaking.
 
Ground water nearby supplies private wells. The surface water is used for recreational purposes. The site
is filled to about 30 feet above the surrounding area. The top and sides of the inactive area of the landfill
are seeded, and vegetation is beginning to grow.
 
Status (June 1984) : EPA is considering various alternatives for the site.
